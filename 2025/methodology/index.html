The California Homebrew Circuit (CHBC) is a free, community-driven initiative connecting homebrewers across the state through friendly competition, shared learning, and cross-club collaboration. This page documents the methodology used for the 2025 CHBC season.

How Competitions Are Selected

To be included in the CHBC standings for 2025, competitions must meet the following criteria:

  • BJCP-Sanctioned: Only competitions sanctioned by the Beer Judge Certification Program (BJCP) are considered.
  • California-Based: The competition must be held within the state of California.
  • Open and Inclusive: Events must be accessible to the broader homebrewing community.
  • Standard Format: Specialty competitions limited to specific styles, ingredients, or themes are not eligible.
  • Results Transparency: Competitions must publicly share complete results using a standardized format.

Each season, we aim to include 10‚Äì12 well-established and geographically diverse competitions. Selection favors events with a history of strong participation, quality judging, and reliable results reporting.

üèÖ Scoring System

The CHBC has adopted a weighted points system that takes into account the size of each competition table. Points are awarded as follows for the 2025 season:

Category Placements

Points are calculated using this formula:

Points = Base Score √ó ((Entries - Place) / Entries)¬≥

Base Scores:

  • 1st place = 3 points
  • 2nd place = 2 points
  • 3rd place = 1 point

This formula rewards higher placements in larger, more competitive tables. For example, earning 1st in a 25-entry table is worth more than 1st in a 6-entry table.

Note: If the total number of entries in a category, table, or flight is not reported or verifiable, a default value of 6 entries will be used for point weighting calculations.

The top three weighted category placements per brewer, per competition, count toward their CHBC total.

Best of Show (BOS)

  • 1st BOS ‚Äì 3 bonus points
  • 2nd BOS ‚Äì 2 bonus points
  • 3rd BOS ‚Äì 1 bonus point

BOS points are awarded separately from category placements. A brewer may earn BOS points even if the winning entry isn‚Äôt among their top three scoring entries.

Data Collection

Results for the 2025 season are gathered from:

  • Publicly posted competition result sheets (PDF, Google Sheets, HTML, etc.)
  • Direct communication with competition coordinators
  • Club announcements and newsletters

Result Standardization

To ensure fairness and consistency across the circuit, all competitions must publish results that:

  • Clearly identify brewers, beer names, BJCP categories/subcategories, and award placements
  • Be available via public link, official website, or verifiable documentation
  • Be posted in a timely manner following the event

CHBC reserves the right to delay or exclude competitions from the 2025 season if results are incomplete, unverifiable, or posted in nonstandard formats.
